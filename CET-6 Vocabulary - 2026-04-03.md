# CET-6 词汇 - 2026-04-03 睡前背诵

## 1. **adversity** /ədˈvɜːsəti/

- **词性**: n.
- **释义**: 逆境，不幸
- **例句**: She showed great courage in the face of adversity.

## 2. **alleviate** /əˈliːvieɪt/

- **词性**: v.
- **释义**: 减轻，缓和
- **例句**: The medicine can alleviate the symptoms of the disease.

## 3. **captivate** /ˈkæptɪveɪt/

- **词性**: v.
- **释义**: 迷住，吸引
- **例句**: The audience was captivated by her performance.

## 4. **compatible** /kəmˈpætəbl/

- **词性**: adj.
- **释义**: 兼容的，合得来的
- **例句**: The software is compatible with most operating systems.

## 5. **consolidate** /kənˈsɒlɪdeɪt/

- **词性**: v.
- **释义**: 巩固，合并
- **例句**: The company consolidated its position in the market.

## 6. **deprive** /dɪˈpraɪv/

- **词性**: v.
- **释义**: 剥夺，使丧失
- **例句**: They were deprived of their basic rights.
- **搭配**: deprive sb. of sth.

## 7. **dilemma** /dɪˈlemə/

- **词性**: n.
- **释义**: 困境，进退两难
- **例句**: She faced a moral dilemma about telling the truth.

## 8. **encompass** /ɪnˈkʌmpəs/

- **词性**: v.
- **释义**: 包含，涵盖
- **例句**: The course encompasses a wide range of topics.

## 9. **exclusive** /ɪkˈskluːsɪv/

- **词性**: adj.
- **释义**: 排他的，专属的，高档的
- **例句**: This is an exclusive club for members only.

## 10. **extravagant** /ɪkˈstrævəɡənt/

- **词性**: adj.
- **释义**: 奢侈的，过度的
- **例句**: They thought the price was extravagant.

## 11. **hypothesis** /haɪˈpɒθəsɪs/

- **词性**: n.
- **释义**: 假说，假设
- **例句**: The scientist proposed a new hypothesis.
- **复数**: hypotheses

## 12. **imperative** /ɪmˈperətɪv/

- **词性**: adj./n.
- **释义**: 必要的，紧急的；祈使语气
- **例句**: It is imperative that we act immediately.

## 13. **indigenous** /ɪnˈdɪdʒənəs/

- **词性**: adj.
- **释义**: 本土的，土生土长的
- **例句**: These plants are indigenous to the tropical regions.

## 14. **lethal** /ˈliːθl/

- **词性**: adj.
- **释义**: 致命的，致死的
- **例句**: The chemical can be lethal if swallowed.

## 15. **prevalent** /ˈprevələnt/

- **词性**: adj.
- **释义**: 流行的，普遍的
- **例句**: Smartphones are now prevalent among young people.

## 16. **reconcile** /ˈrekənsaɪl/

- **词性**: v.
- **释义**: 和解，调和
- **例句**: It's hard to reconcile these two opposing views.

## 17. **resilient** /rɪˈzɪliənt/

- **词性**: adj.
- **释义**: 有韧性的，恢复力强的
- **例句**: Children are often more resilient than adults think.

## 18. **simultaneously** /ˌsɪmlˈteɪniəsli/

- **词性**: adv.
- **释义**: 同时地
- **例句**: The two events occurred simultaneously.

## 19. **spontaneous** /spɒnˈteɪniəs/

- **词性**: adj.
- **释义**: 自发的，自然的
- **例句**: The crowd broke into spontaneous applause.

## 20. **unprecedented** /ʌnˈpresɪdentɪd/

- **词性**: adj.
- **释义**: 史无前例的，空前的
- **例句**: The pandemic caused unprecedented disruption worldwide.
